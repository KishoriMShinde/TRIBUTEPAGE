Chhatrapati SHIVAJI Maharaj


♦ SHIVAJI BHONSALE ( 19 February 1630 – 3 April 1680 ). Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, was an Indian ruler and a member of the Bhonsle Maratha clan. Shivaji carved out his own independent kingdom from the declining Adilshahi sultanate of Bijapur which formed the genesis of the Maratha Empire. In 1674, he was formally crowned the Chhatrapati of his realm at Raigad Fort.

♦ Chhatrapati SHIVAJI Maharaj was born in the hill-fort of Shivneri, near the city of Junnar, which is now in Pune district.

♦ The capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji's empire, the Raigad Fort, stands 2690 feet high above sea level in the Sahyadri mountain range.

♦ Chhatrapati SHIVAJI Maharaj was attributed to have had a control of almost 360 forts in his reign... Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, Maharashtra's greatest warrior king known for his excellent military and guerilla warfare, is often cited as Maharashtra's pride.

♦ Chhatrapati SHIVAJI Maharaj is also known as the Father of Indian Navy. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj built ships in towns such as Kalyan, Bhivandi, and Goa, both for trade and to establish a fighting navy. “He also built a number of sea forts and bases for repair, storage and shelter. Shivaji fought many lengthy battles with Siddis of Janjira on coastline.
